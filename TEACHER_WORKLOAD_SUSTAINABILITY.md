## ğŸ“Š Principle 4: Grade Smarter, Not More

### **The Reality of 6 Courses**

With 120+ students across 6 courses, you CANNOT grade everything perfectly. You don't need to.

**Students Need**:
- Feedback on work (not perfect grades)
- To know where they stand (grade frequency matters more than detail)
- To improve based on feedback (this is the only thing that matters)

**You Don't Need**:
- 100-point grading scales
- Detailed comments on everything
- Perfect rubrics for every assignment
- A grade book with daily updates

### **Smarter Grading Approach**

**System 1: Participation/Engagement (Daily)**
```
How: Clipboard in hand during class
What: Check âœ“, Check minus âœ“-, blank (absent/no work)
Time: 0 minutes (you're already there)
Why: Tracks who's engaged; early flag for problems
Grade Weight: 20-30% of course grade
```

**System 2: Performance Tasks/Projects (Bi-weekly)**
```
How: Students complete project/lab/presentation
What: Use simple rubric (4-point scale: Developing, Proficient, Advanced, Not Ready)
Time: 30-45 min to grade 25-30 students (skim, not perfect review)
Why: Shows what they can actually DO
Grade Weight: 40-50% of course grade
```

**System 3: Quizzes/Assessments (As-Needed)**
```
How: Students take brief quiz (10-15 min, 5-10 questions)
What: Score quickly; focus on patterns not individual points
Time: 15 min to score 25 students
Why: Shows understanding of key concepts
Grade Weight: 20-30% of course grade
```

**System 4: Homework (Optional, Not Always Graded)**
```
How: Assign specific practice/reading
What: Check completion (done/not done); don't grade for perfection
Time: 5 minutes checking (skim)
Why: Reinforces learning; identifies struggling students
Grade Weight: 0-10% (or bonus credit)
```

### **What This Looks Like Practically**

**Example: Electronics & Robotics Course**
- Week 1-2: Circuit Design Project (40 pts)
  - Rubric: Does it work? Is it safe? Can you explain it?
  - 30 min to grade

- Week 2: Daily participation (10 pts)
  - Just check participation âœ“; no detail needed
  - 0 min (automatic)

- Week 3: Circuit Quiz (10 pts)
  - 5 questions, 10 min test
  - 15 min to grade

- Week 4: Homework Check (5 pts)
  - Turn in circuit journal
  - 5 min skim (completion only)

**Total Grading Time for 25 students**: ~50 minutes
**Total Time Spent on Assessment**: 50 min (much less than traditional full-grading approach)
**Student Learning**: Clear, quick feedback driving improvement

## â° Principle 8: The 80/20 Rule

### **80% of Results Come from 20% of Your Effort**

4. **Clear routines** (established once) = runs smoothly all year

**Low-Impact, High-Time Activities** (Do Less):
- âŒ Creating brand new lessons (use templates)
- âŒ Writing detailed comments on every assignment (simple feedback)
- âŒ Perfect rubrics for everything (3-4 good ones reused)
- âŒ Detailed grading of every work (sample-check instead)
- âŒ Email that don't need responses (batch and address together)

**Strategy**: Focus on the 5 high-impact activities. Skip the low-impact ones.
