## üîß Free Tools by Course Area

### **Electronics & Robotics Courses**

#### **Tinkercad** (Free - No Login Required for Basic Use)
- **What**: Online circuit design and 3D modeling
- **Best For**: Circuit simulation, 3D printing design, Arduino programming
- **Access**: https://www.tinkercad.com
- **Your Use**:
  - Students design circuits virtually before building
  - 3D models of enclosures/mounts
  - Arduino simulation lab (code without hardware)
- **Lesson Integration**:
  - "Design a circuit in Tinkercad before you build"
  - "Create a 3D model of your robot housing"
- **Teacher Benefit**: Free, shareable circuits students build, no safety risk for virtual practice

#### **PhET Interactive Simulations** (Free - UC Boulder)
- **What**: Interactive physics/engineering simulations
- **Best For**: Understanding concepts before hands-on
- **Access**: https://phet.colorado.edu
- **Your Use**:
  - Circuit simulations (voltage, current, resistance)
  - Force/motion concepts
  - Energy concepts
- **Lesson Integration**: "Complete this PhET simulation to understand voltage drop, then we'll build it"
- **Teacher Benefit**: Visual learning removes conceptual confusion

#### **YouTube Education Channels** (Free)
- **Channels to Subscribe**:
  - Electronics Creations (circuit tutorials)
  - Paul McWhorter (Arduino, robotics)
  - edX (university-level engineering)
- **Your Use**:
  - Show design process videos
  - Demonstrate advanced builds
  - Student research resource
- **Lesson Integration**: 5-10 min videos as hooks or reinforcement

#### **Arduino Official Resources** (Free, Open Source)
- **What**: Microcontroller programming and projects
- **Best For**: Introduction to programming, robotics, automation
- **Access**: https://www.arduino.cc/en/Guide
- **Your Use**:
  - Free tutorials and example code
  - Large community of shared projects
  - Beginner-friendly programming environment
- **Lesson Integration**: "Here's a sample sketch; modify it for your project"

#### **Fritzing** (Free or Donation)
- **What**: Circuit diagram software
- **Best For**: Documenting student designs, creating handouts
- **Access**: https://fritzing.org
- **Your Use**:
  - Students create professional circuit diagrams
  - You create handouts with proper notation
  - Portfolio documentation for designs
- **Lesson Integration**: "Draw your circuit in Fritzing before building"

## üõ†Ô∏è How to Customize Existing Resources

### **Instead of Creating from Scratch, DO THIS:**

**Step 1: Find Something Close**
- Search: "[your topic] free lesson plan"
- Look on OER Commons, Teachers Pay Teachers (free), your district's shared drive
- Find something that's 70-80% what you need

**Step 2: Adapt, Don't Start Over**
- Same learning objective? Keep it
- Same activity? Modify for your students
- Same assessment? Adjust difficulty/format
- Different context? Replace examples with yours
- **Time**: 30-45 min customization vs. 3 hours creating

**Step 3: Add Your Context**
- "This is what professionals at [local company] do"
- "This is how we'll build it in our shop"
- "Here's where this appears in the real world"
- This makes resources uniquely yours and more engaging

**Example**:
```
Original lesson: "Ohm's Law Calculation"

‚îú‚îÄ Adapted: Added circuit diagrams with specific values from Tinkercad
‚îú‚îÄ Contextualized: "The circuit you're building this week uses this principle"
‚îú‚îÄ Time: 45 min customization
‚îî‚îÄ Result: Professional, customized lesson for your students
```

## üí∞ Where to Find Equipment/Materials Money

### **Grants and Funding** (SMARTER NOT HARDER Approach)

#### **School District Budget**
- **How**: Ask your curriculum supervisor and admin
- **What**: Most districts have small budget for CTE equipment
- **Time**: 10-minute conversation to ask
- **Amount**: $500-5,000/year depending on district

#### **Career Technical Education Grants** (State/Federal)
- **Who**: Hamilton Township School District may already have CTE funding
- **How**: Ask Matthew Sisk‚Äîthese grants are part of CTE supervisor's job
- **Amount**: Often $10,000-50,000/year for entire CTE program
- **Time**: District applies, you benefit

#### **Professional Organization Grants** (ACTE)
- **Organization**: Association for Career and Technical Education
- **Access**: https://www.acteonline.org/grants
- **Amount**: Varies ($500-5,000)
- **Time**: 2-3 hour application
- **Frequency**: Usually annual

#### **Local Business Partnerships**
- **How**: Ask local tech companies, engineering firms, vocational businesses
- **What They Provide**: Equipment donations, guest speakers, mentorship, funding
- **Your Use**: "We're training students in your field; can you support?"
- **Time**: 1-2 conversations

#### **STEM/Robotics Specific Grants**
- **Examples**:
  - VEX Robotics Grant Program: https://www.vexrobotics.com
  - FIRST Robotics Grants: https://www.firstinspires.org
  - Tech-specific grants (Google RISE, Microsoft TEALS)
- **Amount**: Often free equipment + some funding
- **Time**: Application process, but worth it

## üí° Pro Tips

‚úÖ **Do This**:
- Bookmark everything (create organized folders)
- Test tools before using with students
- Check licensing (make sure it's truly free/educational use allowed)
- Start with one tool per course; add as you get comfortable
- Join online CTE communities (Facebook groups, ACTE network)
- Ask other CTE teachers for their favorite free resources

‚ùå **Don't Do This**:
- Assume expensive = better (PhET and Tinkercad rival paid tools)
- Spend hours searching for perfect resource (good enough at 70% is good)
- Use tools without checking student privacy (some free tools sell data)
- Commit to tools you don't have time to learn
- Ignore district/state resources (you may have access already)

## ‚ö° Key Takeaway

**You don't need to create everything from scratch.**

Free, high-quality tools and resources exist for nearly everything you teach. Your job is to:
1. Know where they are (bookmarked)
2. Know how to use them (test once)
3. Customize for your context (30 min work)
4. Teach with them (10 min setup per lesson)

**SMARTER NOT HARDER**: Find something 70% perfect and customize it. It's better than perfect from scratch, and takes 1/5 the time.
