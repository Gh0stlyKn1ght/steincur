## üéØ How to Leverage Matthew Sisk (Practical Strategies)

### **Strategy 1: Standards Alignment Verification**

**Your Goal**: Confirm your 6 courses are teaching the right standards, in the right order, at the right level.

**How to Ask**:
```
"Matthew, I'm developing pacing guides for [course name] for MP3 and MP4.
Can you help me verify I'm aligning to the correct NJSLS standards?
I've organized them by unit and can share my draft for your feedback."
```

**What to Bring**:
- Your draft pacing guide (you already have these!)
- List of units and topics per course
- Lesson objectives you're planning
- Questions about which standards apply (there are hundreds‚Äîhe knows which ones matter for your course)

**Timeline**: Email request 2-3 weeks before you need it; schedule 30-minute meeting to review

**Outcome**: Supervisor confirms alignment and can suggest missing standards or unnecessary ones

---

### **Strategy 2: Curriculum Documentation**

**Your Goal**: Create official curriculum documents that satisfy district requirements and provide clarity.

**How to Ask**:
```
"I'd like to develop formal curriculum guides for my 6 applied technology courses
to ensure consistency with district standards. Can we schedule time to discuss
the format the district prefers and what standards are required?"
```

**What to Bring**:
- Your existing lesson plans
- PACING_GUIDE_STRUCTURE.md (shows how you're thinking about organization)
- List of any questions about standards or sequencing

**Timeline**: This is valuable work that supervisors actively support; can usually get time/guidance within 1-2 weeks

**Outcome**: Supervisor provides template and guidance; you're creating district documentation that protects your work

---

### **Strategy 3: Resource & Technology Recommendations**

**Your Goal**: Get district approval and recommendations for tools, software, and materials you want to use.

**How to Ask**:
```
"I want to incorporate [Tinkercad / PhET / iCEV / specific tool] into my lessons
for [specific course]. Can we discuss whether this aligns with district technology
standards and if there are better alternatives or approved resources?"
```

**What to Bring**:
- Specific tools you want to use
- How they'll be used in lessons
- Cost (free tools are easier to approve)
- Student age appropriateness

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks for review

**Outcome**: Supervisor recommends approved resources, suggests free alternatives, or advises on purchasing

---

### **Strategy 4: Professional Development Planning**

**Your Goal**: Get supervisor support for PDP (Professional Development Plan) that matters to YOU, not just generic requirements.

**How to Ask**:
```
"For my PDP this year, I'd like to focus on [IEP implementation / standards alignment /
student engagement strategies]. Can we plan professional development activities that
address this goal and align with district expectations?"
```

**What to Bring**:
- Your actual teaching challenges (you have IEP_CTE_GUIDE.md, BEHAVIORAL_MANAGEMENT_GUIDE.md‚Äîthese are your PDP focus areas)
- Specific goals for your teaching
- Preferred learning methods (online, in-person, coaching, etc.)

**Timeline**: PDP meetings typically happen early school year; can be updated mid-year

**Outcome**: Supervisor designs PD activities that actually improve your teaching, not just checkbox requirements

---

### **Strategy 5: Course Improvement Based on Data**

**Your Goal**: Use assessment data and student feedback to improve your courses each marking period.

**How to Ask**:
```
"I've analyzed assessment data from [course name] for MP3, and I'm noticing
[specific pattern - e.g., students struggling with theory sections].
Can we review my lesson approach and discuss possible adjustments for MP4?"
```

**What to Bring**:
- Student assessment results (grades, specific standards where students scored lower)
- Your reflection on what worked/didn't work
- Specific areas where students struggled
- Ideas for changes (supervisor helps refine these)

**Timeline**: After each marking period; 30-minute meeting

**Outcome**: Supervisor helps identify root causes and suggests evidence-based adjustments
